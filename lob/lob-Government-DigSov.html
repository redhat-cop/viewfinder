Data Sovereignty

Mandate the onshore residency and processing of all classified data, citizen identity records, and sensitive policy information within national or jurisdictional boundaries. Implement state-approved encryption and ensure key management is exclusively handled by government-vetted personnel or certified local infrastructure. Contracts must explicitly address foreign access risks and guarantee immediate, secure data retrieval under national legal authority.

Technical Sovereignty

Prioritize trusted supplier chains and avoid single-vendor lock-in for mission-critical infrastructure like defense, intelligence, and national health systems. Favor a sovereign cloud or certified hybrid architecture using standardized, open protocols to ensure workload portability and redundancy. Cultivate in-house expertise in system architecture and security to maintain control over the national ICT backbone.

Operational Sovereignty

Maintain direct operational control and logging over all essential public services, including network routing and service configuration. Develop nationally certified incident response teams and maintain parallel capabilities for critical functions like civil defense communications and national identity verification. Mandatory, documented exit strategies must ensure service continuity during provider failure or geopolitical events, prioritizing national resilience.

Managed Services

For outsourced services, retain absolute control over the security baseline, access credentials, and the governance layer. Insist on a "sovereign-by-contract" model specifying that service personnel must be nationally vetted citizens and data processing must be subject solely to the national legal jurisdiction. All service agreements must include provisions for immediate government seizure of data and assets if national security is threatened.

Assurance Sovereignty

Establish the unrestricted right to conduct forensic and security vetting of any external platform used for government services. Mandate accreditation programs that require providers to demonstrate verifiable controls against foreign interference. Utilize trusted national monitoring centers to maintain continuous, independent oversight of the integrity and operational status of all citizen-facing and classified systems.

Executive Oversight

Elevate Digital Sovereignty as a national strategic priority, integrating it with national security and resilience policy. Appoint a senior political or bureaucratic leader to champion the mandate, securing dedicated budget and resources for sovereign-by-design procurement and continuous geopolitical risk assessments tied to digital dependencies.

Open Source

Strategically leverage Open Source Software (OSS) for its inherent transparency and auditability, particularly in security-sensitive areas. Establish a national governance framework to assess and certify OSS components for government use, ensuring they meet national security standards. Actively contribute to and maintain critical OSS projects to mitigate reliance on external development roadmaps and build national software maintenance capability.
